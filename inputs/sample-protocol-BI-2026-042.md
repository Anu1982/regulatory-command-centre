## Protocol title

A Phase III, randomised, open-label, active-controlled study of BI-042 versus an
external control arm in adult patients with previously untreated Stage IV
non-small cell lung cancer expressing PD-L1 (Combined Positive Score ≥ 1).

## 1. Background and rationale

BI-042 is an investigational humanised IgG4 monoclonal antibody targeting the
programmed cell death protein 1 (PD-1) receptor. Non-clinical and early-phase
clinical data suggest a favourable benefit–risk profile in tumours expressing
PD-L1. This Phase III trial is designed to evaluate the efficacy and safety of
BI-042 as first-line therapy in patients with advanced NSCLC whose tumours
express PD-L1 (CPS ≥ 1), a population representing a significant unmet medical
need.

The comparator strategy uses an external control arm constructed from Flatiron
real-world data, informed by the End-of-Phase-2 agreement on the sister
compound BI-0987 (November 2024).

## 2. Objectives

### 2.1 Primary objective

To demonstrate superiority of BI-042 versus the external control arm in
progression-free survival in patients with previously untreated advanced NSCLC
expressing PD-L1 (CPS ≥ 1).

### 2.2 Key secondary objectives

- To demonstrate superiority in overall survival.
- To evaluate objective response rate per RECIST v1.1.
- To characterise the safety and tolerability profile of BI-042.

### 2.3 Exploratory objectives

- To evaluate patient-reported outcomes using EORTC QLQ-C30 and QLQ-LC13.
- To identify predictive biomarkers of response beyond PD-L1 CPS.
- To evaluate pharmacokinetics and immunogenicity of BI-042.

## 3. Study design overview

BI-042 will be administered as monotherapy at 200 mg IV every three weeks until
disease progression, unacceptable toxicity, withdrawal of consent, or completion
of 24 months of treatment, whichever occurs first. The primary analysis will
compare the experimental arm with a contemporaneous external control cohort
constructed from Flatiron real-world data and matched on 12 pre-specified
baseline covariates.

An unblinded sample-size re-estimation is planned at 60% information time to
maintain adequate power in the presence of uncertainty around the expected
effect size. Type I error is preserved through a pre-specified combination test
(Cui-Hung-Wang).

## 4. Study population

### 4.1 Inclusion criteria

Patients are eligible for enrolment if all of the following criteria are met:

1. Age ≥ 18 years at the time of informed consent.
2. Histologically or cytologically confirmed Stage IV non-squamous or squamous
   NSCLC.
3. No prior systemic therapy for advanced or metastatic disease.
4. Tumour PD-L1 expression with Combined Positive Score (CPS) ≥ 1 as determined
   by a central laboratory using an approved immunohistochemistry assay.
5. Eastern Cooperative Oncology Group (ECOG) performance status of 0 or 1.
6. Measurable disease per RECIST v1.1 as determined by the investigator.
7. Adequate organ function per Appendix A of the protocol.
8. Written informed consent obtained prior to any study-specific procedure.

### 4.2 Exclusion criteria

Patients are excluded if any of the following apply:

1. Prior treatment with any anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, or anti-CTLA-4 therapy.
2. Active or previously documented autoimmune disease requiring systemic
   treatment within the past two years.
3. Known symptomatic central nervous system metastases.
4. Active infection requiring systemic therapy.
5. Prior organ transplantation, including allogeneic haematopoietic stem cell
   transplantation.
6. Any medical or psychiatric condition that, in the opinion of the
   investigator, would preclude safe participation.

### 4.3 Rationale for the biomarker cutoff

The CPS ≥ 1 cutoff is chosen to align with the broader indication label sought
for BI-042. Sponsor acknowledges that the three most recent FDA approvals in
first-line advanced NSCLC used a CPS ≥ 10 cutoff for the primary analysis. This
protocol currently applies a single-cutoff analysis at CPS ≥ 1; a hierarchical
testing strategy (CPS ≥ 10 → all-comers) is under consideration but not yet
adopted.

## 5. Objectives and endpoints

### 5.1 Primary endpoint

Investigator-assessed progression-free survival (PFS), defined as the time from
randomisation to the first documented disease progression per RECIST v1.1 or
death from any cause, whichever occurs first.

### 5.2 Key secondary endpoints

- Overall survival (OS), defined as the time from randomisation to death from
  any cause. Sponsor notes that recent EMA CHMP opinions in this indication
  have consistently preferred OS as primary or co-primary; this is under
  active review by the Regulatory Strategy team.
- Objective response rate (ORR) per RECIST v1.1.
- Safety endpoints per NCI-CTCAE v5.

### 5.3 Exploratory endpoints

- Patient-reported outcomes (EORTC QLQ-C30, QLQ-LC13).
- Duration of response (DOR).
- Time to next treatment (TTNT).
- Pharmacokinetics (Cmax, Cmin, AUC).

## 6. Study design details

### 6.1 Randomisation and stratification

Not applicable to the ECA-based primary comparison. Sensitivity analyses using
a small internal randomised concurrent control (~ 40 patients) are under
discussion following EOP2 feedback on the sister compound BI-0987.

### 6.2 Adaptive design elements

Unblinded sample-size re-estimation at 60% information time. Type I error is
controlled through a pre-specified combination test with early-stopping
boundaries defined in the Statistical Analysis Plan. An independent Data
Monitoring Committee firewall separates the sample-size re-estimation team from
the study team.

### 6.3 Blinding

The study is open-label. Radiological assessments are performed centrally by
Blinded Independent Central Review (BICR) as a key secondary analysis to
mitigate assessment bias in the primary endpoint.

## 7. Investigational product and comparator

### 7.1 Investigational product

BI-042 is supplied as a sterile, preservative-free liquid concentrate for
intravenous infusion, 200 mg in single-dose vials. Administered as a 30-minute
IV infusion every three weeks.

### 7.2 Comparator — external control arm

The comparator is an external control arm constructed from Flatiron
electronic health record data. The cohort is matched to the experimental arm on
12 pre-specified baseline covariates: age, sex, ECOG performance status,
smoking history, histology (squamous vs non-squamous), stage at diagnosis,
liver metastases, brain metastases, PD-L1 CPS category, lactate dehydrogenase,
haemoglobin, and calendar year of diagnosis.

Sponsor is aware of the recent FDA Complete Response Letter to a competitor
programme in which residual confounding in the external control precluded a
reliable estimate of comparative effectiveness. The current protocol relies
solely on the external control; the addition of a small concurrent randomised
sensitivity arm is under discussion.

### 7.3 Concomitant therapy

Standard supportive care is permitted throughout the treatment period.
Palliative radiotherapy to non-target lesions is permitted at investigator
discretion. Systemic corticosteroids > 10 mg prednisone equivalent daily are
generally not permitted except for the management of immune-related adverse
events.

## 8. Study procedures

Screening within 28 days of randomisation. Treatment cycles are 21 days.
Tumour assessments per RECIST v1.1 are performed every 6 weeks for the first
12 months, then every 12 weeks thereafter. Safety assessments are performed
at every visit. Survival follow-up continues until study end.

## 9. Statistical considerations

### 9.1 Estimand framework

Consistent with ICH E9(R1), the primary estimand is defined as follows:

- **Treatment condition:** BI-042 versus external control.
- **Population:** Patients with previously untreated advanced NSCLC with
  PD-L1 CPS ≥ 1 meeting all inclusion criteria and no exclusion criteria.
- **Endpoint:** Progression-free survival per RECIST v1.1.
- **Intercurrent event strategies:**
  - Treatment discontinuation: **treatment-policy** strategy.
  - Treatment switching to subsequent therapy: **hypothetical** strategy.
- **Population-level summary:** Hazard ratio and 95% confidence interval.

A sensitivity analysis using a while-on-treatment strategy for the safety
estimand is planned in the Statistical Analysis Plan.

### 9.2 Sample size

Approximately 420 patients in the experimental arm, matched to 420 patients in
the external control arm, provide at least 90% power to detect a hazard ratio
of 0.70 at a one-sided α of 0.025, assuming an event rate of 60% at the primary
analysis time. Sample size is re-estimated at 60% information time.

### 9.3 Multiplicity

A graphical Bonferroni-based multiplicity procedure controls the family-wise
error rate across the primary endpoint (PFS) and the key secondary endpoints
(OS, ORR). The graph and associated α propagation rules are pre-specified in
the Statistical Analysis Plan.

### 9.4 Analysis populations

- **Full Analysis Set (FAS):** All randomised patients (experimental arm) and
  all matched ECA patients.
- **Safety Analysis Set:** All patients who received at least one dose of
  BI-042.
- **Per-Protocol Set:** FAS excluding patients with major protocol deviations.

### 9.5 Interim analyses

One unblinded interim analysis is planned at 60% information time for the
purpose of sample-size re-estimation. No efficacy stopping is planned at the
interim.

## 10. Safety and data monitoring

### 10.1 Adverse event reporting

Adverse events are graded per NCI-CTCAE v5 and reported per ICH E2A/E2F. All
serious adverse events are reported to the sponsor within 24 hours.

### 10.2 Data Safety Monitoring Board

An independent Data Safety Monitoring Board (DSMB) reviews unblinded safety
data at pre-specified intervals. The DSMB charter defines the following:

- **Membership:** Three voting members (medical oncology, biostatistics,
  clinical safety) with no significant conflict of interest with the sponsor.
- **Independence:** DSMB members have no operational role in the trial.
- **Meeting cadence:** Every six months, or ad hoc following pre-specified
  safety triggers.
- **Stopping rules:**
  - **Futility:** Predictive probability of success < 10% at the interim
    analysis.
  - **Efficacy:** Only after the primary analysis time.
  - **Toxicity:** Pre-specified triggers for immune-related adverse events,
    including grade 4 events in > 5% of patients within any 6-month window.

### 10.3 Risk mitigation

Immune-related adverse event management guidelines are provided in Appendix B
and are aligned with current SITC and ESMO consensus recommendations.

## 11. Ethics and regulatory considerations

The study is conducted in accordance with ICH-GCP, the Declaration of Helsinki,
and all applicable local regulations. Independent Ethics Committee or
Institutional Review Board approval is obtained prior to any study-specific
procedure at each site. Written informed consent is obtained from each patient
prior to any study-specific procedure.

## 12. Data handling and quality

Electronic data capture is via a validated system compliant with 21 CFR Part 11
and Annex 11. Central monitoring and risk-based site monitoring are performed
throughout the study. All data are handled in accordance with applicable
privacy regulations (GDPR, HIPAA).
